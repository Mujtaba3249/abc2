Figure 1 — Context Diagram (DFD Level 0)

The context diagram shows the Topaz Jewelry Platform as a single process (0) and its data exchanges with all external entities — clients, the admin team, partner workshops, the payment gateway, the notification service, and the shipping carrier. Each external entity is drawn with a stacked-paper outline; arrows are labelled with the data that flows along them.

Figure 2 — Data Flow Diagram (Level 1)

Level 1 decomposes the Topaz system into its seven core processes (1.0–7.0) and shows the data stores (D1–D6) they use. Numbered processes are drawn as rounded rectangles; data stores use the D-prefix box; external entities keep the stacked-paper style.

Figure 3 — Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)

Chen-style ERD for the Topaz platform. Rectangles are entities, ovals are attributes (underlined oval = primary key, double oval = multi-valued, dashed oval = derived, parenthesised = optional), diamonds are relationships. Cardinality is shown with the standard 1 / M / N notation on each connecting line.

UML activity diagram for the end-to-end client journey on Topaz: from registration through browsing, custom design (with admin approval), checkout, payment, workshop production, and delivery. Black bars are fork/join nodes; diamonds are decisions; rounded rectangles are activities.
